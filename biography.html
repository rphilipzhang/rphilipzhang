    Experiences

    Pre-College

    I was born and raised in Chengdu, a city famous for its beautiful scenery, delicious food, and long history. Spending my first 18 years in one of the most livable cities in China, I enjoyed my childhood very much. More importantly, I really appreciate my parents' tremendous efforts in inspiring my intellectural curiosities in appreciating and creating knowledge, which later become the driving force of my potential as a professional researcher. From September 2001 to June 2007, I attended Chengdu Experimental Foreign Languages School (CEFLS) for my high-school study. During my study in CEFLS, I was (and still am) fascinated by the beauty of mathematics and physics, and by their compelling power to understand our God-made world. I won several prizes in contests in mathematics and physics, one of which was the first prize in Chinese Mathematics Olympiad. It was this prize that enabled me to waive the national college entrance exam and get admitted to the Department of Information Management, Peking University. In September 2007, I left Chengdu and became an undergraduate student in Peking University.

    Peking University

    After the freshman year of my undergraduate study at Peking University, I haved decided to advance my study in mathematics. As a result, in June 2008, I transferred to School of Mathematical Sciences, Peking University. During my undergraduate years, I focused my study on pure mathematics, with particular emphasis on analysis and probability. I also did some research on discrete geometry, number theory, and geometry of numbers under the supervision of Professor Chuanming Zong. From June 2009 to September 2010, my research on the discrete generalization of Aleksandrov's Projection (Uniqueness) Theorem was supported by the National University Student Innovation Program of China (the final paper of this project).

    Throughout my last one and a half years of my undergraduate study, an intriguing question kept puzzling me: how to use mathematics to make better decisions? For the search of the answer to this question, I have put my main academic efforts to probability theory (and its applications) and operations research. I joined my B.S. thesis advisor, Professor Ke Liu's research group in May 2010, and became a regular speaker in the group seminar until I left Beijing for Ph.D. study in July 2011. Under the supervision of Professor Liu, I finished my B.S. thesis on a comprehensive review of the health care operations literature in June 2011. During my study and research in Professor Liu's group, I made up my mind to devote my career to the discipline of operations research and operations management.

    During my undergraduate study, I also worked for several educational NGOs (e.g. China Rural Library and Beijing Western Sunshine Rural Development Foundation) in China, trying to help the students from the rural area of China. I help these organizations manage and serve volunteers as well as work as a volunteer myself.

    Washington University in St. Louis

    From August 2011 to May 2016, I was pursuing my PhD degree in the Operations and Manufacturing Management Group at Olin Business School, Washington University in St. Louis. My coursework training at WashU is multi-facet, including optimization, stochastic process, statistics, machine learning, microeconomics and econometrics. My academic advisors at Olin Business School are Professor Nan Yang and Professor Fuqiang Zhang. I have also worked with my classmate Guang Xiao, as well as Professor Long Gao from UC Riverside and Ting Luo from Cal State Fullerton. My research focuses on developing models and effective methodologies to address some fundamental operations issues (pricing, inventory control, capacity allocation, and procurement) under the emerging trends in technology, marketplace, and society (social networks, sustainabililty concerns, customer behaviors, and supply risks). My research has been published in Operations Research and Manufacturing & Service Operations Management. My paper has also won the finalist award in the POMS College of Supply Chain Management 2015 Best Student Paper Competition. You are more than welcome to read and download my papers on my Research Page.

    I did a lot of TA works during my doctoral study at Olin Business School. For example, I work with Professor Sergio Chayet in his MBA course "Operations Management" in each semester, and with Professor Bernardo Silveira in his undergraduate course "Market Competition and Value Appropriation" in each fall semester. I have also TAed Professor Fuqiang Zhang's PhD course "Research Topics in Operations and Supply Chain Management" in Spring 2015. Please refer to my Teaching Page for more about my teaching.

    New York University Shanghai

    Since August 2016, I have been working at New York University Shanghai as an Assistant Professor of Operations Management. I am particularly enthusiastic about the following research topics: online platforms and marketplaces, sharing economy, social networks, and sustainability. I teach the undergraduate Business Analytics and Operations Management courses at NYU Shanghai. I also consult in retailing and new engergy vehicle industries.

    In my spare time, I enjoy staying with my family and serving the God.
